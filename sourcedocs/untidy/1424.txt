Most of Iraq is a series of broad, sandy plains. The Syrian Desert blankets
the land in the extreme west. In the southeast near the Persian Gulf, the land
is low, marshy, and often flooded.

Iraq is mountainous in the north, as the Zagros Mountains front its borders
with Iran and Turkey. The country's highest point is located there, an unnamed
peak rising 11,847 ft (3,611 m).

Two of the most significant rivers in all of Asia, the Euphrates and Tigris
Rivers, drain the land.
