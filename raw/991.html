      One thing you may notice when you visit Stockholm for the first time is that many people are very fashionable, and that many apartments and shops have very distinctive and exceptionally beautiful interior designs. Stockholmers love nice things and many of them are quite serious about what and where they buy their various items. Despite its rather small size, the city is a great destination for shopping and this guide will lead you to the places that should be at the top of your list.

      cafespecific's City Tips

      • Get inspiration from what Stockholmers wear! You will be on the safe side by following the popular trends of the city.
      • Shopping in Stockholm can be quite expensive. Check out the seasonal sales for awesome bargains!
      • Some of the more expensive Swedish brands have factory outlets either in the city center or on the outskirts of Stockholm.
      • Old Haymarket
        Flea & Street Markets
        See more photos
        cafespecific Says:

        The Hay Market in Stockholm is a market place in the city center where you can buy fresh food, flowers, antiques (sometimes), and much more. In addition to the market square itself, there is a cinema, a concert hall and a very cool department store. Start your shopping tour here to get some initial inspiration.

        Good for
        • Foodie
        • Must See
        • LGBT
        • Trendsetter
        • Like a Local
        • Solo
        • Friends
        • Couples
        • Senior
        • Shopping Fanatic
        • Family
        • Art and Architecture Lover
        Tips
        • Prices magically shrink right before the market closes. Be patient if you want to save some money!
        • Visit Hötorgshallen when you are at the Hay Market. Just go down the stairs next to cinema to experience one of Stockholm's coolest underground food markets.
        • Check out PUB, the department store located right next to the Hay Market. PUB offers designer fashion, furniture, and much much more!
        • The sculptures in front the the concert hall are actually created by the famous artist, Carl Milles.
        Plan to stay:         Less than 1 hour

      • Drottninggatan
        Gift & Specialty Shops
        See more photos
        cafespecific Says:

        Drottninggatan (Queen Street) is Stockholm's main shopping street. The street stretches from the Royal Palace on Gamla Stan to Observatorielunden; a small hill from where you get a great view over the city. The street itself attracts many locals and visitors with a great variety of shops and restaurants.

        Good for
        • Must See
        • Like a Local
        • Solo
        • Friends
        • Couples
        • Senior
        • Shopping Fanatic
        • Family
        Tips
        • Come here after visiting the Hay Market. Drottninggatan is right next to it!
        • One of the main shopping attractions on Drottninggatan is Åhléns City, the biggest branch of the Swedish department store chain Åhléns.
        • This street is better for shopping than for eating. If you are hungry, instead of going to a restaurant on Drottninggatan, visit one down one of the side streets.
        Plan to stay:         1-2 hours

      • Kulturhuset
        Civic Centers
        See more photos
        cafespecific Says:

        Kulturhuset (the Culture House) is a theater/café/performance center in the city center of Stockholm. It is located right across from Åhléns City and is the perfect place to continue your shopping tour. The square in front of Kulturhuset leads you to Sergelgången, an underground shopping street that connects Åhléns with Nordiska Kompaniet.

        Good for
        • Solo
        • Friends
        • Couples
        • Senior
        • Shopping Fanatic
        • Family
        • Art and Architecture Lover
        Tips
        • Check out Design Torget on the ground floor of Kulturhuset. In this shop you'll find unique Swedish gifts, kitchen equipment, and other fun things.
        • There is a great coffee shop on the 7th floor of Kulturhuset. Here, not only do you get awesome Swedish cake and coffee, but also a great view over the city.
        Plan to stay:         1-2 hours

      • Gallerian
        Gift & Specialty Shops
        cafespecific Says:

        Stockholm can be pretty cold, not only during winter, but also in spring and autumn. Because of this, the city offers many indoor shopping opportunities, such as Gallerian, a shopping mall in the city center. Gallerian is located right next to Kulturhuset and is a great place for shopping on a rainy or snowy day. In this mall you will find the most famous Swedish clothing store chains, such as Topshop/Topman, Hollister, as well as a bunch of restaurants and coffee shops.

        • This mall offers free WiFi.

      • Nordiska Kompaniet (NK)
        Department Stores
        See more photos
        cafespecific Says:

        Nordiska Kompaniet is a luxury department store in central Stockholm. Often referred to as NK, Nordiska Kompaniet have managed to not only impress their customers with the variety and quality of products they offer, but also with their artistic display of goods. The department store is known for its extravagant shop windows and is a great place to visit even if you don't want to buy anything.

        Tips

        • There is a beautiful, hip coffee shop called Espresso House on the lower floor of NK.
        • There are several traditional Swedish restaurants and cafés on the upper floors.

      • Ostermalm Saluhall
        Flea & Street Markets
        See more photos
        cafespecific Says:

        Östermalm Saluhall is an indoors food market, close to Sturegallerian. The market was inaugurated in 1888 and has since attracted many locals and tourists for both grocery shopping and dining. In addition to vegetables and foods from all over the world, the market offers a variety of gourmet restaurants and food stalls.

      • Svenskt Tenn
        Gift & Specialty Shops
        See more photos
        cafespecific Says:

        Svenskt Tenn is a furniture store on Strandvägen, specializing in colorful and unique Swedish designs. The store opened in 1924 and is still popular among Stockholmers today. There really is no other store like Svenskt Tenn, so if you need new furniture or accessories for your apartment, this is the place to go.

        Good for

        • Trendsetter
        • Like a Local

        • Family
        Tips
        • The quality and uniqueness of what you get at Svenskt Tenn is reflected in the price. Make sure to bring some money if you plan on buying anything.
        • This is a great place to buy gifts!

      • Emmaus Vintage Shop
        Gift & Specialty Shops
        cafespecific Says:

        Emmaus Vintage Shop is a great place for all bargain hunters. There are several vintage stores in Stockholm, but none of them are quite as cool as Emmaus. The store is located close to Götgatan, the hipster shopping street of Södermalm. The fashion here is very representative of the alternative/hipster style of Södermalm and a great place to get a typical Stockholm outfit.

      • Stockholm Quality Outlet Barkarby
        Factory Outlets
        cafespecific Says:

        This is one place in Stockholm that thrifty travelers shouldn't miss. The Quality Outlet in Barkaby offers pretty much all Swedish high-end brands at outlet prices. The outlet is located outside the city center but can be easily reached by the commuter train and bus.

        Good for

        • Couples

        Tips
        • There is an Ikea right next to the outlet, just in case you urgently need a scented candle.
        • If you want to try Swedish fast food, try Max Burgers across the street from the main entrance. The food is actually very good!
