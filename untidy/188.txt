# Alive

From Biology-Online Dictionary | Biology-Online Dictionary

Jump to: navigation, search

Alive

    1. Having life, in opposition to dead; living; being in a state in which the organs perform their functions; as, an animal or a plant which is alive. 

    2. In a state of action; in force or operation; unextinguished; unexpired; existent; as, to keep the fire alive; to keep the affections alive. 

    3. Exhibiting the activity and motion of many living beings; swarming; thronged. _The Boyne, for aquarter of a mile, was alive with muskets and green boughs._ (Macaulay) 

    4. Sprightly; lively; brisk. 

    5. Having susceptibility; easily impressed; having lively feelings, as opposed to apathy; sensitive. _Tremblinglyalive to natures laws._ (Falconer) 

    6. Of all living (by way of emphasis). _Northumberland was the proudestman alive._ (Clarendon) 

Used colloquially as an intensive; as, man alive!

alive always follows the noun which it qualifies.

Origin: oe. On live, as. On life in life; life being dat. Of lif life. See
life, and cf. Live.
